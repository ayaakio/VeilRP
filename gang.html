Faction Guidelines

Starting a Gang

  • When starting a gang, players must open a general ticket with the gang name, member list, and intended location of operation.
  • VeilRP follows a “what you own is what you hold” process, so gangs should plan ahead around the real estate surrounding their chosen location.
  • MLOs are a privilege, not a right. Gangs must show strong roleplay over time before being offered an MLO.
  • Criminal organisations must remain within the roleplay style of their group type. For example, motorcycle clubs should mainly use motorcycles.
  • Leaders are responsible for keeping their roster updated and are accountable for the actions of their group.
  • If staff believe a group is negatively impacting the server, they may disband the group.

Expectations

  • All criminal organisations are expected to have an active player base across a 7 day period.
  • Organisation leaders must notify staff if they will be on LOA for more than 7 days. Members must remain active during this time.
  • Groups must notify staff of disbandment or roster changes for documentation purposes.
  • All groups must follow server rules at all times and promote good roleplay in all interactions.
  • Roleplay comes before gunplay. Arena mentalities and toxic behaviour are not acceptable.
  • Groups are expected to create positive, long-term roleplay and use a “yes, and” mindset.

Tier System

Tier 1

  • Can hold turf under turf rules, but cannot push out a higher tier group already established there.
  • Can start conflict, but cannot take part in war.
  • Can represent colours, but not custom clothing.

Tier 2

  • Can hold turf but are not entitled to an MLO.
  • Can use custom clothing brought into the server or made on request, depending on availability.
  • Can start conflict, take part in wars, and set war terms.
  • Can get a group vehicle, as long as it follows TOS.

Tier 3

  • Includes all Tier 1 and Tier 2 perks.
  • Can receive an MLO or gang warehouse.
  • Tiers can be given or removed by staff depending on behaviour, reports, and overall roleplay quality.
  • Moving up a tier is a privilege, not a right. Staff have final say.

Tier Notes

  • The tier system exists to provide structure between multiple criminal organisations and reward strong roleplay.
  • Staff may increase or reduce a gang’s tier depending on behaviour, reports, and the overall standard of roleplay seen on the server.
  • Tier progression is never automatic and is always at staff discretion.

Turf Rules

  • All criminal organisations may hold a location of their choosing, but must buy real estate in that area to officially own it as turf.
  • If a group does not own the location, they cannot claim it as turf and may be easily pressed off it by others.
  • Groups that buy real estate in an area may, at Tier 3, be able to replace it with an MLO depending on availability and developer approval.
  • If an MLO is not available, the location may instead be decorated.
  • Groups may defend official turf with members already present at the location when hostilities begin.
  • Groups may not call in extra reinforcements unless they are below the 5 person limit.
  • Groups pushing turf must stay within the 5 person limit, even if the defending side already has more present because it is their turf.
  • This protection only applies to the compound itself, not the surrounding neighbourhood, unless the location is a city-recognised compound.
  • Groups may not abuse turf protection by baiting conflict, roaming in large numbers, or provoking fights on another gang’s turf.
  • Turf may only be taken by Tier 2 or Tier 3 groups when there is proper roleplay reasoning.
  • “Because we can” is never valid reasoning for taking turf.
  • Turf may only extend one block around the main location. Groups cannot claim full regions of the city.

Conflict Rules

  • Groups may hit a rival group once per storm unless the other side retaliates.
  • If Group A hits Group B and Group B does not retaliate, Group A must wait until the next storm.
  • If Group B retaliates, the conflict may continue back and forth between the two groups.
  • Conflict may only last 5 total days. If no resolution is reached, a war ticket must be opened in the official ZeroPulse Discord.
  • War tickets must include reasoning, terms, any additional information, and the intended resolution.
  • If both sides cannot agree to terms, staff may end the conflict.
  • Groups involved in conflict may not call allied groups for support.
  • The conflict must remain strictly between the two involved groups.
  • Faction members must wear colours or identifying clothing linked to their gang during active fights.
  • The only exception is when members are actively trying to black out and the conflict is brought onto them.
