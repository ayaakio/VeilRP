General Rules

Activity Requirement

  • Businesses must remain active. If found inactive for 14+ days, ownership status may be reviewed.
  • Owners must regularly operate and sell products or services.
  • If owners are only benefiting from money without actively engaging with the company, ownership may be reviewed.
  • Staff may request proof of activity through sales logs, employee rosters, and similar records.
  • Ghost businesses may be revoked.

Ownership Responsibility

  • Business owners are responsible for employee conduct.
  • Owners must ensure employees follow all server rules.
  • Owners must prevent exploit abuse within their business.
  • Failure to manage staff properly may result in business penalties.

Economic Balance

  • Businesses must operate within server economy guidelines.
  • Prices should stay within a reasonable margin compared to similar businesses.
  • Extreme undercutting or price gouging to sabotage competitors is not allowed.
  • No artificial inflation.
  • No abuse of scripts to generate excessive profit.
  • Any business found abusing mechanics may be removed from the owner and sold elsewhere.
  • Staff may review pricing if it disrupts server balance.

Fair Competition

  • No unfair monopolising of essential services.
  • Businesses must allow others to enter the market.
  • Blocking other players from entering a business market is not allowed.
  • Healthy competition is allowed. Sabotage is not.

Audits and Reviews

  • Staff may review businesses if economic imbalance is suspected.
  • Staff may review businesses if exploits are reported.
  • Staff may review businesses if complaints are received.
  • Staff may review businesses if activity drops significantly.
  • Possible outcomes include warnings, adjustment requests, temporary freezes, or license removal.

Menu/Product Limits

  • Businesses must follow item limits relevant to their category.
  • Excessive duplication of items through reskins or renames to bypass limits is not allowed.
  • Staff approval may be required for unique or custom items.

Other

  • Owning a business is a privilege, not a right.
  • Business ownership should promote positive and realistic roleplay at all times.
  • Business ownership is intended to reward active players.
  • Staff decisions regarding approvals, tiers, and reviews are final.

Tier System

  • The tier system below applies only to food-based restaurant businesses and other smaller businesses.
  • It does not apply to mechanics, retail shops, dealerships, or similar larger business types.
  • The tier system exists to prevent menu spam, reward active businesses, control crafting and item bloat, and encourage progression.

Tier Progression Rules

  • To progress through tiers, businesses must show consistent owner activity.
  • Businesses must demonstrate regular sales and active operation by staff or employees.
  • Businesses may need to submit a tier upgrade request if required by staff.
  • Staff may downgrade tiers if activity drops, rules are broken, or menu abuse occurs.

Tier 3

  • Maximum 6 total menu items. Food, drinks, and combos all count toward the total.
  • Designed for food trucks, small cafés, and new restaurants.
  • All restaurants begin at this rank.
  • To move upward, businesses must stay active and contribute positively to server roleplay.

Tier 2

  • Maximum 9 total menu items. Food, drinks, and combos all count toward the total.
  • Requires demonstrated active operation.
  • Requires consistent sales.
  • Requires staff approval.
  • To move upward, businesses must remain active and positively contribute to the community.

Tier 1

  • Expanded menu flexibility, including specialty or seasonal items.
  • Requires strong and consistent activity.
  • Requires staff approval.
  • Tier 1 is a privilege, not a default.
  • Promotion depends on roleplay quality, community contribution, and overall activity.
